EvaCun2025

The first project I got involved in was the EvaCun2025 Shared Task on Lemmatization of Ancient Cuneiform Languages, a workshop associated with the Ancient Language Processing sector of the NAACL conference. I worked with two colleagues on this task, Brandi Wilkins and John Sbur, under the guidance of Dr. Yudong Liu. This task involved using ChatGPT 4.0-Mini to lemmatize ancient cuneiform languages, which we did through the use of data processing and prompt engineering. I worked on the implementation of data processing infrastructure, performed data analysis, and helped build our conceptual model based off of successes in previous research by Dr. Liu as well as our own ideas.

We submitted our paper to the conference, and were invited to attend in April of 2025. At this conference, we got a chance to present our work and connect with other academics to learn about their methodologies on the same project. We attended talks and events to learn about new research and publications in the fields of AI and NLP. This experience introduced me to the world of academic research, and also helped me inform my own views on hot topics in the CS industry, like AI and ML. Our source code can be found here.

DARLing Lab

Since April 2025, I have been working as a member of the DARLing lab (Documentation, Analysis, and Revitalization), I work with the Hän language under the guidance of Maura O'Leary. We are working to preserve this endangered language and expand access to language-learning materials. I am part of the Transducer team, where we work on maintaining a morphological transducer that breaks a Hän word into its English counterpart and inflectional morphemes, or takes an English word and attaches the inflectional morphemes to form the proper Hän word. This maintenance involves the entry of data from a dictionary into the transducer through the use of lexd, as well as checking the format of existing and new entries to the transducer. We also analyze verb paradigms to add verb entries to the transducer, using different data from the charts to assess which morphological rules can apply to the given verb.

Since September 2025, I have worked as a lab manager with the DARLing lab, I have been helping onboard new members and prepare them for the research assistant role. I have also been working on documentation, making sure that we have guides and FAQ documents for various parts of the lab to help speed up the onboarding process.

KIND Lab

In December 2025, I joined Dr. Yasmine Elglaly's KIND Lab, which focuses on accessibility in computing. The project I work on within the lab is using research-based strategies to develop an educational game called You&I for use by speech and language pathology practitioners with young clients diagnosed with ASD, specifically children that experience pronoun reversal. I have worked with Tyanin Opdahl to finish a paper investigating prior research and presenting her previously conducted survey, which resulted in design recommendations from speech language pathology professionals and other professionals that work with young autistic individuals, such as Special Education teachers in elementary schools. Currently, I work with Tyanin and Ryan Critchlow to conduct a pilot study in which the first level of the implemented game is being tested with young children with ASD, which eventually will result in recommendations for our next steps, which will shape how future implementation of the game goes before the final result is published. I am working on this project as my Senior Research capstone for the CS Department with my colleagues Kayla Warner and Rizon Takabe. We will be helped rewrite the code and design and implement 2-3 new levels alongside several minigames. We will write a paper and present our findings, which will pertain to how educational games can help young children with autism in their speech development, and what features are important for educational games with this focus.
